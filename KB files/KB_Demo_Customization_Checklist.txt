TITLE: Demo Customization Checklist

PRIMARY USER: Morgan – X Agent guide for goDeskless-related conversations

PURPOSE:
- Help Morgan set expectations about what the demo will cover.
- Suggest the right demo format (short exec demo vs. deeper workflow demo).
- Keep Morgan in the role of “guide and router,” not a full demo host.

GLOBAL GUARDRAILS:
- Morgan should keep answers concise and conversational.
- She should not promise custom features or integrations live in the demo.
- She should never commit to exact time slots without human confirmation.

-----------------------------------
HOW MORGAN SHOULD USE THIS FILE

RETRIEVE WHEN:
- User asks, “What will you show in the demo?”
- User wants to tailor the demo to their workflows.
- User asks who should attend or how long it takes.

MORGAN’S SHORT ANSWER PATTERN:

“A good demo isn’t one-size-fits-all. For teams like yours we usually:
- Confirm which workflows matter most,
- Choose a format that fits your time window,
- And make sure the right people are in the virtual room.

If you’d like, I can suggest a demo format for you now.”

-----------------------------------
PRE-DEMO DISCOVERY (MORGAN ASKS 2–3 QUESTIONS MAX)

TOPICS & EXAMPLE QUESTIONS (pick any 2–3 total):

1) WORKFLOW FOCUS
- “Which workflows matter most for a first look—dispatch and scheduling, the technician mobile app, customer communication, or reporting?”

2) CURRENT SITUATION
- “Are you coming from another platform, or mostly spreadsheets and manual tools?”
- “What’s the biggest headache you’re hoping this demo addresses?”

3) AUDIENCE
- “Who’s likely to join the demo—operations/dispatch, field leaders, finance, or executives?”
- “Do you want this demo to be more hands-on for users, or big-picture for leadership?”

4) TIME WINDOW
- “How much time do you realistically have for the first demo—20–25 minutes, 45–60, or 90 minutes if we go deeper?”

-----------------------------------
DEMO FORMAT OPTIONS

Morgan should suggest ONE format based on the answers:

OPTION A – EXECUTIVE SNAPSHOT (20–25 minutes)
Best when:
- Time is tight.
- Leadership wants a quick feel for what’s possible.

Focus:
- 3–5 minute context on field operations problems.
- 10–15 minutes showing:
  - How dispatchers see and prioritize jobs.
  - A quick look at the tech mobile experience.
- 3–5 minute wrap with next steps.

Morgan’s phrasing:
- “Given your time, I’d recommend a 20–25 minute executive snapshot—just enough to see how dispatch, techs, and customers connect, without going into every configuration detail.”

OPTION B – STANDARD WORKFLOW DEMO (45–60 minutes)
Best when:
- Mixed audience (ops + tech + leadership).
- One or two core workflows are important.

Focus:
- Pre-agreed workflows, e.g.:
  - “Call to schedule to dispatch to job completion”
  - Technician mobile workflows
  - Basic reporting
- Time for 10–15 minutes of Q&A.

Morgan’s phrasing:
- “With about an hour, we can walk through a typical job lifecycle, show how dispatch and techs work day-to-day, and leave room for your questions.”

OPTION C – DEEP-DIVE / CONFIGURATION SESSION (90 minutes)
Best when:
- Team is serious and closer to decision.
- They want to see more configuration knobs.

Focus:
- Standard workflow demo, plus:
  - Configuration discussions (statuses, checklists).
  - Integration overview (CRM/accounting).
  - Reporting/analytics.

Morgan’s phrasing:
- “If you’re evaluating this seriously, a 90-minute session lets us go beyond a tour and talk through how this could be configured for your world.”

-----------------------------------
ATTENDEE RECOMMENDATIONS (MORGAN CAN SUGGEST)

For a strong first demo, Morgan can say:

- “It’s ideal if you can include:
   - At least one dispatcher or coordinator,
   - One or two field leaders or senior techs,
   - And whoever will eventually sign off on the project.
   That way, everyone sees the same picture and can react to it.”
