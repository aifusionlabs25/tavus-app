# KB_14: IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE & DEPLOYMENT PROCESS

**Purpose:** Provide Morgan with realistic timelines, deployment phases, and common questions about onboarding, training, and go-live for goDeskless implementations.

---

## STANDARD IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE

**Typical Duration**: 6-8 weeks (mid-market operations, 20-100 technicians)  
**Fast-Track Option**: 4 weeks (small operations, <20 technicians, minimal integrations)  
**Enterprise Option**: 10-12 weeks (100+ technicians, complex CRM integrations, multi-location rollout)

---

## PHASE-BY-PHASE BREAKDOWN

### **Phase 1: Discovery & Planning (Week 1-2)**

**Goal**: Understand current workflows, system requirements, and success criteria.

**Activities**:
- **Kickoff call** with goDeskless implementation team + customer stakeholders (Operations, IT, Finance)
- **Workflow mapping**: Document current dispatch, scheduling, invoicing, and reporting processes
- **Data audit**: Review existing customer data, technician profiles, service history (for migration)
- **Integration planning**: Confirm CRM connectors (Salesforce, ServiceNow, HubSpot, etc.) and API requirements
- **Success metrics**: Define KPIs (e.g., first-time fix rate, truck roll reduction, technician utilization)

**Customer Responsibilities**:
- Assign an internal project manager (usually VP Ops or Service Manager)
- Provide access to existing system data (CSV exports, API credentials)
- Identify pilot group (5-10 technicians for initial rollout)

**Timeline**: 1-2 weeks

---

### **Phase 2: Configuration & Data Migration (Week 3-4)**

**Goal**: Configure goDeskless platform, migrate customer/technician data, and integrate with existing systems.

**Activities**:
- **Platform setup**: Configure dispatch rules, service territories, technician skill sets, job types, pricing templates
- **CRM integration**: Connect Salesforce, ServiceNow, HubSpot, or other CRM (native connectors)
- **Data migration**: Import customer profiles, service history, equipment records, technician profiles
- **Mobile app customization**: Configure digital checklists, job forms, custom fields
- **Testing environment**: Set up sandbox environment for pilot group testing

**Customer Responsibilities**:
- Provide clean data exports (CSV or API access)
- Review/approve platform configuration
- Assign pilot technicians (5-10 users for initial testing)

**Timeline**: 2 weeks

---

### **Phase 3: Training & Pilot (Week 5-6)**

**Goal**: Train internal teams (dispatchers, field supervisors, technicians) and run pilot program.

**Activities**:
- **Admin training** (2-3 hours): Dispatch console, scheduling, reporting, mobile technician management
- **Technician training** (1-2 hours): Mobile app walkthrough, job completion, customer signatures, photo uploads
- **Pilot launch**: 5-10 technicians run live jobs using goDeskless for 1-2 weeks
- **Feedback loop**: Daily check-ins with pilot users to identify issues, UX friction, or workflow gaps
- **Iteration**: Adjust configurations, mobile forms, or dispatch rules based on pilot feedback

**Customer Responsibilities**:
- Participate in live training sessions (virtual or on-site)
- Collect pilot user feedback and share with goDeskless implementation team
- Monitor pilot performance (job completion rates, mobile adoption, data accuracy)

**Timeline**: 1-2 weeks

---

### **Phase 4: Full Rollout & Go-Live (Week 7-8)**

**Goal**: Deploy goDeskless to all technicians and transition from legacy system.

**Activities**:
- **Full deployment**: Roll out goDeskless mobile app to all technicians
- **Legacy system cutover**: Migrate remaining workflows from old system to goDeskless
- **Go-live support**: goDeskless team provides live support (phone/chat) during first week of full deployment
- **Post-launch monitoring**: Track adoption metrics, data quality, integration health
- **Final training**: Office hours for Q&A, advanced features, reporting deep dives

**Customer Responsibilities**:
- Communicate rollout plan to all field teams
- Decommission or sunset legacy system
- Monitor adoption and escalate issues to goDeskless support

**Timeline**: 1-2 weeks

---

### **Phase 5: Optimization & Success Review (Week 8-12)**

**Goal**: Fine-tune workflows, optimize dispatch algorithms, and validate ROI.

**Activities**:
- **Performance review**: Analyze KPIs (first-time fix rate, truck roll reduction, technician utilization)
- **Workflow optimization**: Adjust AI dispatch rules, service territories, or job types based on real-world usage
- **Advanced feature enablement**: Roll out SnapHelp video assist, AI analytics, or custom reporting
- **Quarterly business review**: Executive debrief with customer leadership (VP Ops, CFO) to validate ROI

**Customer Responsibilities**:
- Provide feedback on platform performance
- Identify additional use cases (e.g., warranty tracking, contract management)
- Plan for scale (expand to new service lines, additional locations)

**Timeline**: 4 weeks (ongoing optimization)

---

## FAST-TRACK IMPLEMENTATION (4 WEEKS)

**Best For**: Small operations (<20 technicians), minimal CRM integrations, simple workflows

**Condensed Timeline**:
- **Week 1**: Discovery + Configuration (combined)
- **Week 2**: Data Migration + Training
- **Week 3**: Pilot (3-5 technicians)
- **Week 4**: Full Rollout

**Trade-offs**:
- Less customization (use out-of-the-box configurations)
- Minimal data migration (start fresh vs. importing legacy data)
- Shorter training windows (1-hour sessions vs. 2-3 hours)

---

## ENTERPRISE IMPLEMENTATION (10-12 WEEKS)

**Best For**: 100+ technicians, multi-location, complex CRM integrations, custom workflows

**Extended Timeline**:
- **Week 1-3**: Discovery & Requirements Gathering (multi-stakeholder workshops)
- **Week 4-6**: Configuration, API Integrations, Data Migration
- **Week 7-8**: Pilot (10-20 technicians, multiple locations)
- **Week 9-10**: Phased Rollout (region-by-region or department-by-department)
- **Week 11-12**: Full Deployment + Optimization

**Additional Considerations**:
- **Security reviews**: SOC 2, GDPR compliance validation
- **Change management**: Internal communications, executive sponsorship, technician buy-in campaigns
- **Custom integrations**: ERP systems, legacy databases, third-party APIs
- **Multi-location coordination**: Staggered rollouts across regions/branches

## COMMON QUESTIONS & MORGAN'S RESPONSES

### **"How long does it take to get up and running?"**
*"Most teams go live in 6-8 weeks. If you're a smaller operation with minimal integrations, we can fast-track it to 4 weeks. If you're enterprise-scale with complex CRM setups, we plan for 10-12 weeks. The key is starting with a pilot group—usually 5-10 technicians—so we can test workflows and refine before full rollout."*

---

### **"Do we need to migrate all our historical data?"**
*"Not necessarily. Some teams migrate everything (customer profiles, service history, equipment records), while others start fresh and keep legacy data in an archive. It depends on your reporting needs and contract obligations. We can export CSV files from most systems and import them into goDeskless in a few days."*

---

### **"What if our technicians resist the new system?"**
*"Great question—that's the #1 concern we hear. The good news: goDeskless is mobile-first, so technicians typically adopt it in 2-3 days because it's simpler than legacy tools. We also recommend a pilot program where 5-10 'champion technicians' test it first, give feedback, and then help train their peers. That peer-to-peer influence is way more effective than top-down mandates."*

---

### **"Can we integrate with our existing CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.)?"**
*"Absolutely. We have native connectors for Salesforce, ServiceNow, Microsoft Dynamics, HubSpot, and Zoho. Most CRM integrations are configured during Week 3-4 of implementation. If you're using a custom or legacy CRM, we also have RESTful APIs for custom integrations—though those take a bit longer (usually 2-4 weeks of dev work)."*

---

### **"What kind of training do you provide?"**
*"We offer live virtual training (or on-site if needed). Dispatchers and admins get 2-3 hours of training on the console, scheduling, and reporting. Technicians get 1-2 hours on the mobile app—job workflows, digital checklists, customer signatures, photo uploads. We also provide on-demand video tutorials and a help center for ongoing reference."*

---

### **"What happens if something breaks during go-live?"**
*"We provide live support during your first week of full deployment—phone, chat, and email. Our implementation team monitors your system health, data sync, and integration status daily. If issues arise, we troubleshoot in real time. Most go-lives are smooth because we've already tested everything during the pilot phase."*

---

### **"Can we roll out to one location first, then expand?"**
*"Definitely. Many multi-location operations do a phased rollout—one branch or region at a time. That lets you validate workflows, refine training, and build internal champions before scaling company-wide. It also reduces risk and gives your team time to adjust."*
