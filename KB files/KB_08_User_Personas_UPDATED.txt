## PRIMARY END-USER PERSONAS (Original 5)

### **1. VP OF OPERATIONS / COO** (Economic Buyer)

**Profile:**
- **Role**: Oversees field service operations, responsible for P&L, operational efficiency, customer satisfaction
- **Company Size**: Mid-market to enterprise (50-500+ employees)
- **Reports To**: CEO / President
- **Direct Reports**: Field Service Managers, Dispatchers, Customer Service Teams

**Pain Points:**
- **Cost per service call is too high** (excessive truck rolls, overtime, fuel costs)
- **Low first-time fix rates** (technicians don't have right parts/info, leading to repeat visits)
- **Poor technician utilization** (manual dispatch, inefficient routing, downtime between jobs)
- **Customer complaints** (missed appointments, slow response times, lack of visibility)
- **Inability to scale** (current systems break down as team grows)

**Goals:**
- Reduce operational costs (truck rolls, labor, fuel)
- Improve customer satisfaction (NPS, retention)
- Scale operations without hiring more dispatchers
- Get real-time visibility into field operations (GPS tracking, job status, KPIs)
- Justify ROI to CFO/CEO (payback period, cost savings, revenue growth)

**goDeskless Value Prop:**
- **20-30% reduction in truck rolls** via SnapHelp video assist
- **15-25% improvement in first-time fix rates** (AI dispatch matches right technician to right job)
- **6-12 month payback period** (proven ROI from case studies)
- **Real-time operational dashboard** (GPS tracking, job status, technician performance)

**Morgan's Approach:**
- Lead with ROI and cost savings
- Use case study examples from KB_10
- Ask about current cost per service call, technician utilization, customer satisfaction metrics
- Position goDeskless as a **profit center, not a cost** (operational efficiency = revenue growth)

### **2. FIELD SERVICE MANAGER / OPERATIONS MANAGER** (Operational Buyer)

**Profile:**
- **Role**: Manages day-to-day dispatch, scheduling, and field team performance
- **Company Size**: Small to mid-market (10-100 technicians)
- **Reports To**: VP Operations / COO
- **Direct Reports**: Dispatchers, Field Supervisors, Technicians

**Pain Points:**
- **Manual dispatch is chaos** (Excel spreadsheets, whiteboards, phone calls)
- **Constant firefighting** (last-minute schedule changes, emergency calls, technician no-shows)
- **No visibility into field teams** (Where are my technicians? Are they on schedule? Did they complete the job?)
- **Technician pushback on new tools** (complex software, poor mobile UX)
- **Reporting is a nightmare** (manually compiling data for executives)

**Goals:**
- Automate dispatch and scheduling
- Real-time visibility into field operations
- Easy-to-use tools that technicians actually adopt
- Automated reporting for executives
- Reduce time spent on admin tasks (more time coaching teams)

**goDeskless Value Prop:**
- **AI-powered "BestMatch" dispatch** (right technician, right job, right time—automatically)
- **Real-time GPS tracking** (see all technicians on a map, live job status)
- **Mobile-first design** (technicians adopt it in 2-3 days)
- **One-click reporting** (job completion rates, technician performance, customer feedback)

**Morgan's Approach:**
- Emphasize **ease of use** and **technician adoption** (biggest fear is new tool complexity)
- Ask about current dispatch process, pain points, time spent on admin tasks
- Offer demo focused on dispatch workflow and mobile technician experience

### **3. FIELD TECHNICIAN** (End User)

**Profile:**
- **Role**: Front-line service delivery (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, appliance repair, facilities maintenance)
- **Company Size**: Any (solo technician to enterprise field teams)
- **Reports To**: Field Service Manager / Supervisor
- **Tools**: Mobile app, GPS, van stock inventory, customer communication

**Pain Points:**
- **Clunky mobile apps** (slow, crashes, poor UX)
- **Manual paperwork** (service reports, invoices, customer signatures on paper)
- **Unclear job instructions** (missing customer history, equipment details, special requests)
- **Poor GPS directions** (wrong addresses, inefficient routing)
- **No offline mode** (rural areas with spotty cell coverage)

**Goals:**
- Simple, fast mobile app (get in, do the job, get out)
- All job info in one place (customer history, equipment details, checklist)
- Digital paperwork (no manual forms, instant invoicing)
- Accurate GPS and routing
- Offline mode for rural jobs

**goDeskless Value Prop:**
- **Technicians adopt it in 2-3 days** (mobile-first, intuitive design)
- **All job info in one app** (customer history, equipment records, digital checklists)
- **Digital signatures and invoicing** (paperless, instant email receipts)
- **Accurate GPS routing** (integrated with Google Maps, optimized for efficiency)
- **Offline mode** (complete jobs in areas with no cell coverage, sync when back online)

**Morgan's Approach:**
- Focus on **simplicity and speed** (technicians care about efficiency, not features)
- Show mobile app demo (job workflow, digital checklist, customer signature)
- Highlight **offline mode** and **paperless invoicing**

### **4. IT MANAGER / CIO** (Technical Buyer)

**Profile:**
- **Role**: Oversees enterprise technology stack, security, integrations, data governance
- **Company Size**: Mid-market to enterprise (100-1,000+ employees)
- **Reports To**: CIO / CTO / CFO
- **Direct Reports**: IT Support, Security, Database Admins

**Pain Points:**
- **Integration complexity** (vendor APIs are poorly documented, custom integrations take months)
- **Security and compliance risks** (data breaches, GDPR violations, SOC 2 audits)
- **Vendor lock-in** (legacy systems with proprietary data formats, hard to migrate)
- **Poor API documentation** (developers waste time troubleshooting)
- **Shadow IT** (business units buying software without IT approval)

**Goals:**
- Seamless CRM integration (Salesforce, ServiceNow, HubSpot, Dynamics)
- SOC 2 compliance, GDPR readiness
- API-first architecture (easy to integrate with existing systems)
- Fast deployment (6-8 weeks, not 6-12 months)
- Data portability (no vendor lock-in, own your data)

**goDeskless Value Prop:**
- **Native CRM connectors** (Salesforce, ServiceNow, HubSpot, Dynamics, Zoho)
- **SOC 2 certified, GDPR compliant** (enterprise-grade security)
- **RESTful APIs** (developer-friendly, well-documented)
- **6-8 week deployment** (vs. 6-12 months for legacy FSM platforms)
- **API-open, data portability** (you own your data, easy to export)

**Morgan's Approach:**
- Lead with **security and compliance** (SOC 2, GDPR)
- Highlight **CRM integrations** and **API documentation**
- Ask about current tech stack, integration requirements, security concerns
- Offer to connect with goDeskless technical team for deep dive

### **6. PROCUREMENT / VENDOR MANAGEMENT** (Gatekeeper)

**Profile:**
- **Role**: Manages vendor onboarding, contract negotiations, MSA/SOW review
- **Company Size**: Enterprise (500-5,000+ employees)
- **Reports To**: CFO / COO
- **Direct Reports**: Procurement Specialists, Contract Managers

**Pain Points:**
- **Vendor onboarding paperwork** (W-9, insurance certificates, security questionnaires)
- **Contract compliance** (MSAs, SLAs, data processing agreements)
- **Budget constraints** (CFO pressure to reduce software spend)
- **Vendor consolidation** (too many point solutions, prefer platform plays)

**Goals:**
- Streamline vendor onboarding (fast, compliant, minimal back-and-forth)
- Negotiate favorable contract terms (pricing, SLAs, exit clauses)
- Ensure vendor compliance (security, insurance, legal)
- Reduce total cost of ownership (consolidate vendors)

**goDeskless Value Prop:**
- **SOC 2 certified** (security questionnaires already completed)
- **Standard MSA templates** (legal review-ready)
- **Transparent pricing** (no surprise fees, clear SLA terms)
- **Fast onboarding** (vendor setup in 1-2 weeks)

**Morgan's Approach:**
- Acknowledge procurement requirements early: "Do you have a formal vendor onboarding process?"
- Offer to provide security documentation, MSA templates, insurance certificates
- Position goDeskless as **procurement-friendly** (fast, compliant, transparent)

### **7. EXECUTIVE SPONSOR / CHAMPION** (Power Buyer)

**Morgan's Approach:**
